Computational Geospatial Science

I develop open-source geospatial techno-economic models for assessing the viability of different Internet broadband connectivity options. I apply spatial optimization models including Steiner Trees to design and plan broadband networks to estimate maximum capacity coverage and minimize costs and carbon emissions.

Current projects!

These are my current Projects, focusing on geospatial models for evaluating different broadband connectivity opetions including Low Earth Orbit satellites. The models are simplified to ensure that anyone can customize, use their data to produce the results. Users can also contribute to the repositories to extend the functionalities and application in specific areas.

  • Mobile Broadband Geospatial Model

    With over 2.6 billion still unconnected to the Internet, new mobile technology systems including 5G and 6G will be crucial in narrowing digital divide, thanks to higher penetration of smartphones. This model evaluates and compares the capacity, coverage, cost and environmental impact of connecting the unconnected using 4G and 5G.

  • Broadband Connectivity and Climate Change Resilience

    While there has been a greater focus on quantifying the relationship between poverty and natural hazard vulnerability, no existing analysis quantifies these effects when also considering cellphone coverage (given cellphones provide a strong mitigation strategy via early warning messages). This is further compounded by the lack of open source geospatial data on the number of unconnected people at a global scale [13]. One proxy for quantifying the number of unconnected users, is information on those regions uncovered by existing mobile cellular technologies, such as 2G, 3G or 4G.

  • Fixed Fiber Broadband

    Fixed fiber provides an option for connecting some of the unconnected areas through innovative network layout such as Fiber-to-the Neighborhood (FTTnb) architecture. Unlike satellite, fixed fiber is considered as one of the greenest forms of broadband capable of providing descent speeds. Here an open source integrated geospatial model is developed for estimating the capacity, coverage, cost and emissions of fixed fiber broadband systems in connecting the population below the poverty line and uncovered population in rural areas.
